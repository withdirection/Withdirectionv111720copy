# WITHdirection Brand Guidelines

## About WITHdirection

WITHdirection is a Deaf-led sign language interpreting and accessibility consulting firm based in Brooklyn, NY, founded by Christopher Tester. With 15+ years of experience, the firm provides professional interpreting services from Brooklyn's cultural institutions to the United Nations, serving multinational, multicultural, and multilingual clients.

---

## Brand Identity

WITHdirection is:
- **Deaf-led** – Centering Deaf expertise in all work
- **Research-informed** – Grounding practice in professional standards
- **Institutional-level** – Built for professional, organizational settings
- **Boutique** – Personalized service with expert coordination

---

## Messaging Pillars

### Deaf Expertise
Deaf-led expertise and lived linguistic knowledge as central to access.

### Professional Standards
Research-informed practice and real-world interpreting experience.

### Strategic Access
Communication access as a professional service requiring planning.

### Intentional Coordination
Teams coordinated by expertise and long-term relationships.

### Multilingual Practice
Recognizes and advances diversity of sign languages globally.

---

## Language to AVOID

❌ **Never use:**
- "Eliminating communication barriers"
- "Helping Deaf people overcome limitations"
- "Giving Deaf people a voice"
- Inspiration narratives or deficit framing
- Generic business clichés ("solutions," "seamless," "empower")

✅ **Instead use:**
- Strategic language (intentional, coordinated, research-informed)
- Professional service language (expertise, planning, infrastructure)
- Respectful framing that centers Deaf knowledge

## Design System

### Typography
- **Headings:** Montserrat
- **Body text:** Fira Sans
- Modern, clean hierarchy with generous spacing

### Colors
- **Primary Navy:** `#14213D`
- **Accent Blue:** `#00A9E0`
- **Accent Purple:** `#CB6CE6`
- White and grays for contrast

### Style Principles
- Modern, professional aesthetic appropriate for institutional clients
- Accessibility-first design
- Clean layouts with intentional whitespace
- Avoid overly rounded corners or dated web 2.0 styling
- Use asymmetric, dynamic layouts over centered/symmetrical ones

### Voice & Tone
- Professional and confident
- Research-informed, not academic
- Direct and purposeful
- Respectful of Deaf expertise and community

## Notes for Content Development

When creating any content for WITHdirection:
1. Lead with Deaf expertise, not deficit narratives
2. Frame access as strategic planning, not problem-solving
3. Emphasize coordination and intentionality
4. Use precise, professional language
5. Avoid clichés and inspirational framing
6. Center institutional-level service delivery
